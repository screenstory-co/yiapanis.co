<p>An exploration of French New Wave techniques and philosophy, with particular attention to <em>Les Bonnes femmes</em> (1960). The movement's influence on contemporary filmmaking — jump cuts, location shooting, natural light, and the democratisation of the camera.</p>

<p>The French New Wave (<em>La Nouvelle Vague</em>) broke with tradition in 1959–1964. Directors like Chabrol, Truffaut, Godard and Rivette rejected studio-bound production in favour of lightweight equipment, real locations, and non-professional actors. The handheld camera became a tool of authorship — an extension of the director's eye rather than a fixed industrial apparatus.</p>
